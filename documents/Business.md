# Daily Dose - Business Strategy

**Status:** Active  
**Last Updated:** April 2, 2026

## Purpose

This document defines the business model, strategic constraints, and commercial priorities for Daily Dose.

It should keep product and engineering decisions aligned with margin, retention, and long-term sustainability.

## Business Objective

Daily Dose is intended to become a lean, high-margin digital product business that can support the owner without requiring a large team or heavy operational overhead.

Target outcome:

- durable recurring revenue
- low fixed cost base
- strong product trust
- sustainable founder workload
- founder net income greater than 200,000 per year after business expenses, tax planning, retained profit, and reinvestment

## Strategic Positioning

Daily Dose sits between:

- daily puzzle games
- brain training products
- asynchronous social ritual products

The differentiation is structural:

- shared daily ritual
- premium mastery path
- social competition without live-service chaos
- a deeper multi-game portfolio than single-mechanic daily products
- stronger habit value through insight, progression, and social warmth

## Product Ambition

The product ambition is to compete directly with the best-known daily word and logic games, then exceed them on:

- breadth of high-quality game systems
- replay value through controlled variants
- social utility and warmth
- habit strength and emotional reward
- premium value without trust erosion

Target long-term portfolio:

- 10 core games
- up to 3 live variants per game
- a standard player track and a richer playtest track

## Revenue Model

Primary revenue should come from subscriptions, not ads.

Principles:

- no pay-to-win mechanics
- no dark patterns
- no monetization that damages trust in fairness

### Core paid offer

Forge Mode is the main premium offer.

It should provide:

- personalized challenge
- deeper progression
- archive access
- richer stats and insight
- stronger identity for committed players
- the ability to create tribes, subject to anti-spam and safety rules

### Pricing intent

The intended price band remains annual-first, with monthly as a secondary option.

Current target range:

- AUD 39 to AUD 59 per year

Monetization is still exploratory. Near-term strategy should be:

- validate habit and retention before aggressive pricing work
- test premium packaging before adding secondary monetization
- keep ads off the core gameplay path unless unit economics later force reconsideration

## Unit-Economics Mindset

The product should be designed so costs stay flat relative to engagement growth.

Cost controls:

- batch AI generation
- model competition with deterministic filtering before ranking
- deterministic rejection before human review
- no default per-user content generation
- no real-time infrastructure requirement
- sampled deep telemetry instead of full retention of raw activity
- automated operating loops so the founder is not manually supervising daily content at scale

## Growth Strategy

Growth should be retention-led, not acquisition-led.

Primary loops:

- daily sharing
- streak momentum
- social invitation through tribes
- periodic seasonal hooks
- warm co-play and discussion between friends, couples, families, and groups

The product should spread because the ritual is compelling, not because the funnel is aggressive.

## Strategic Risks

Highest risks:

- building too many systems before one habit loop is proven
- allowing AI convenience to weaken puzzle quality
- shipping social features that add complexity without return
- growing surface area faster than the codebase and docs can support
- collecting sensitive user data without a clear value, consent model, or compliance plan
- building manipulative reward systems that damage trust or trigger platform, policy, or legal problems
- creating a 10-game vision without an operating model that can ship calmly

## Data and Trust Boundary

Daily Dose may use profile and behavioral data to improve relevance, segmentation, and product quality, but must not cross into exploitative or careless data use.

Rules:

- profile collection must be optional, clearly explained, and tied to user value
- collect coarse and useful data, not maximum possible data
- do not require sensitive demographics for core play
- do not use protected traits to demean, exclude, or algorithmically disadvantage users
- do not use precise location by default
- tribe, ranking, and social systems must motivate without humiliating users
- analytics and insight should help users feel progress, mastery, and connection, not addiction by design

## Business Rules

Use these rules to reject weak ideas quickly:

1. If it does not improve retention, it should not ship.
2. If it increases cost without increasing trust or value, reject it.
3. If it complicates the daily ritual, simplify first.
4. If premium value is unclear in one sentence, the offer is not ready.
5. If the feature cannot be operated calmly, it is too expensive even if the cloud bill is low.
6. If the feature depends on invasive data collection, it must earn that cost explicitly.
7. If a mechanic increases engagement by undermining trust, reject it.

## Near-Term Commercial Priorities

Current priorities, in order:

1. Prove a compelling daily habit.
2. Prove that the product can be operated mostly by automation with calm human oversight.
3. Prove that the UI can be improved quickly and safely.
4. Establish a credible premium path through Forge Mode.
5. Add social systems only when they clearly increase return.

## Long-Term Expansion

Possible future expansion areas:

- seasonal content packs
- workplace tribes
- premium analytics
- curated special events

These should only be pursued once the core daily product has reliable retention and a stable operating model.
