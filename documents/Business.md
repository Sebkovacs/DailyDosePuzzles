# Daily Dose - Business Strategy

**Status:** Active  
**Last Updated:** April 2, 2026

## Purpose

This document defines the business model, strategic constraints, and commercial priorities for Daily Dose.

It should keep product and engineering decisions aligned with margin, retention, and long-term sustainability.

## Business Objective

Daily Dose is intended to become a lean, high-margin digital product business that can support the owner without requiring a large team or heavy operational overhead.

Target outcome:

- durable recurring revenue
- low fixed cost base
- strong product trust
- sustainable founder workload

## Strategic Positioning

Daily Dose sits between:

- daily puzzle games
- brain training products
- asynchronous social ritual products

The differentiation is structural:

- shared daily ritual
- premium mastery path
- social competition without live-service chaos

## Revenue Model

Primary revenue should come from subscriptions, not ads.

Principles:

- no pay-to-win mechanics
- no dark patterns
- no monetization that damages trust in fairness

### Core paid offer

Forge Mode is the main premium offer.

It should provide:

- personalized challenge
- deeper progression
- archive access
- richer stats and insight
- stronger identity for committed players

### Pricing intent

The intended price band remains annual-first, with monthly as a secondary option.

Current target range:

- AUD 39 to AUD 59 per year

## Unit-Economics Mindset

The product should be designed so costs stay flat relative to engagement growth.

Cost controls:

- batch AI generation
- deterministic rejection before human review
- no default per-user content generation
- no real-time infrastructure requirement
- sampled deep telemetry instead of full retention of raw activity

## Growth Strategy

Growth should be retention-led, not acquisition-led.

Primary loops:

- daily sharing
- streak momentum
- social invitation through tribes
- periodic seasonal hooks

The product should spread because the ritual is compelling, not because the funnel is aggressive.

## Strategic Risks

Highest risks:

- building too many systems before one habit loop is proven
- allowing AI convenience to weaken puzzle quality
- shipping social features that add complexity without return
- growing surface area faster than the codebase and docs can support

## Business Rules

Use these rules to reject weak ideas quickly:

1. If it does not improve retention, it should not ship.
2. If it increases cost without increasing trust or value, reject it.
3. If it complicates the daily ritual, simplify first.
4. If premium value is unclear in one sentence, the offer is not ready.
5. If the feature cannot be operated calmly, it is too expensive even if the cloud bill is low.

## Near-Term Commercial Priorities

Current priorities, in order:

1. Prove a compelling daily habit.
2. Prove that the UI can be improved quickly and safely.
3. Establish a credible premium path through Forge Mode.
4. Add social systems only when they clearly increase return.

## Long-Term Expansion

Possible future expansion areas:

- seasonal content packs
- workplace tribes
- premium analytics
- curated special events

These should only be pursued once the core daily product has reliable retention and a stable operating model.
