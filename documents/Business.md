# **Daily Dose — Business Strategy & Execution Plan**

***

## 1. Business Objective

Daily Dose is designed to become a **lean, high‑margin digital product business** that generates:

*   **$200k+ AUD net annual income** to the owner
*   with low fixed operating costs
*   no large team dependency
*   and no growth‑at‑all‑costs pressure

The strategy prioritises:

*   retention over acquisition,
*   clarity over complexity,
*   and compounding value over viral spikes.

***

## 2. Market Positioning

### Category

Daily Dose sits at the intersection of:

*   daily puzzle games,
*   brain training,
*   and social ritual products.

It competes indirectly with:

*   Wordle‑style dailies
*   NYT Games
*   standalone puzzle apps

Its differentiation is structural, not cosmetic.

***

### Core Positioning Statement

> **Daily Dose is the only puzzle platform that combines  
> a universal daily challenge, personal mastery paths,  
> and seasonal social competition — without rushing, grinding, or burnout.**

***

## 3. Revenue Model Overview

Daily Dose is built around **subscription‑first economics**.

No ads.  
No pay‑to‑win.  
No dark patterns.

Revenue comes from **value depth**, not user volume.

***

## 4. Primary Revenue Stream — Membership

### 4.1 Premium Membership (Forge Mode)

**Product**

*   Personalised Forge Mode
*   Full puzzle archive
*   Advanced stats and insight
*   Bonus variants
*   Enhanced tribe participation

**Pricing Strategy**

*   Annual‑first (default)
*   Psychological range:
    *   $39–$59 AUD per year (target)
*   Monthly option as secondary anchor

**Why this works**

*   Annual pricing improves cash flow
*   Reduces churn
*   Encourages long‑term habit formation
*   Aligns with the daily ritual concept

***

### 4.2 Revenue Target Math (Key)

To achieve **$200k AUD net**, we assume:

*   \~20–25% operational cost ratio
*   Required gross revenue: \~$260k AUD

This can be achieved with:

*   \~4,500 users × $59/year  
    **or**
*   \~6,500 users × $39/year

This is a **very realistic scale** for a global daily puzzle product with strong retention.

***

## 5. Secondary Revenue Streams (Optional but Powerful)

These are **not required** for viability, but materially increase upside.

***

### 5.1 Seasonal Events

Limited‑duration puzzle seasons:

*   special puzzle sets
*   unique rules or themes
*   cosmetic rewards

Monetised via:

*   premium access
*   event passes
*   bundle upgrades

Benefits:

*   short‑term revenue spikes
*   re‑engagement of lapsed users
*   marketing hooks

***

### 5.2 Workplace Tribes (B2B‑Lite)

Offer Daily Dose as:

*   a team brain‑break
*   light wellness tool
*   friendly competition platform

**Pricing**

*   Per‑seat monthly or annual
*   $3–$7 AUD per user / month

Even modest adoption creates meaningful revenue due to:

*   low churn
*   minimal support overhead
*   predictable billing

***

### 5.3 Education Packs (Long‑Term)

Selective alignment with:

*   schools
*   tutoring platforms
*   enrichment programs

Only explored once core B2C product is stable.

***

## 6. Cost Structure & Margin Protection

### 6.1 Fixed Costs (Low)

*   Hosting & serverless infrastructure
*   Authentication & database
*   AI API usage (batched, capped)
*   App store / distribution fees

No payroll.
No office.
No real‑time infra.

***

### 6.2 Variable Costs (Controlled)

*   AI generation occurs in batches, not per user
*   Deterministic validation prevents rework
*   No per‑user puzzle generation by default
*   Limited deep telemetry retention

The architecture is explicitly designed to **flatten costs as usage grows**.

***

## 7. Growth Strategy (Retention‑Led)

Daily Dose does **not** rely on paid acquisition to succeed.

***

### 7.1 Organic Growth Loops

*   Daily sharing of Universal scores
*   Tribe events prompting invites
*   Seasonal competition announcements
*   Streak and completion milestones

These are *built into the product*, not bolted on.

***

### 7.2 Controlled Virality

Daily Dose spreads via:

*   conversations (“Did you solve today’s Vault?”)
*   friendly challenges
*   group participation

Sharing is:

*   optional
*   pride‑based
*   spoiler‑safe

***

### 7.3 App Store & Web Presence

Focus on:

*   clarity of value
*   elegance of screenshots
*   explaining the *ritual*, not features

***

## 8. Operational Model (Solo‑Founder Safe)

### Daily

*   Review analytics summary
*   Approve scheduled puzzles
*   Monitor errors

### Weekly

*   Review evaluator feedback
*   Tune difficulty bands
*   Plan content batches

### Monthly

*   Assess retention
*   Adjust pricing or packaging
*   Ship one meaningful improvement

The system is designed so:
**consistency > heroics**.

***

## 9. Risk Management

### Key Risks

*   Over‑reliance on AI output
*   Over‑building social systems
*   Feature creep before retention

### Mitigations

*   Deterministic validation layer
*   Asynchronous competition only
*   Clear build order
*   Document‑driven development

***

## 10. Long‑Term Vision

Daily Dose aims to become:

*   a trusted daily habit
*   a recognised puzzle brand
*   a calm alternative to noisy games
*   a financially independent product business

Success looks like:

*   steady daily usage
*   strong subscription renewal
*   organic growth
*   and a product you still enjoy running years from now

***

## 11. Business Rule Summary

*   **If it does not improve retention, it does not ship.**
*   **If it increases cost without increasing trust, it is rejected.**
*   **If it complicates the system, simplify first.**
*   **If it cannibalises the daily ritual, don’t add it.**
